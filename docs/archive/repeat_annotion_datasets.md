# Curated TE ground-truth datasets for angiosperm genomes

## Executive summary

**The strongest, EDTA-independent ground-truth resources for benchmarking CARP are, in order: (1) the TREP/ClariTeRep wheat annotation on IWGSC RefSeq v1.0 (Wicker lab, manual, Wicker-2007 canonical), (2) the PGSB/TREP barley annotation on Morex V3, (3) BeetRepeats v1.0 for sugar beet (Schmidt/Heitkam lab, 2024), (4) SoyTEdb for Williams 82 soybean, (5) the Jiao 2017 / Stitzer 2021 structural B73 RefGen_v4 TE annotation for maize combined with the Wessler–Bennetzen MTEC library, (6) TAIR10 TE annotation (Buisine/Quesneville 2008) plus Repbase `athrep.ref` for Arabidopsis Col-0, and (7) RiTE-db + RetrOryza + the MSU7/RAP-DB annotation for Nipponbare rice.** These are the resources where manual curation (or long-standing community consensus) is best documented, they are distributed in open or low-barrier repositories, and they are demonstrably independent of the EDTA pipeline.

Second-tier but useful complements are **Repbase Update** (subscription) and the **curated "DF" subset of Dfam 3.9** (open; ~300 plant families), both of which provide manually curated family-level consensuses that underlie most other plant TE resources; **RepetDB** (URGI/INRAE, REPET-based with transparent evidence) for Brachypodium, grape, apple, tomato, cacao, Medicago; and **REXdb/GyDB** for LTR retrotransposon lineage-level reference. For MITEs specifically, **P-MITE** (2014) remains the best multi-species MITE catalogue.

The dominant risk the user asked us to guard against is pervasive: **essentially every plant pan-genome and T2T paper published since ~2020 has adopted EDTA or panEDTA as the TE annotator**, including the Hufford 2021 NAM maize paper, Ou 2024 panEDTA maize, Lian 2024 and Kang 2023 Arabidopsis pan-genomes, Shang 2022 / Qin 2021 / Wu 2023 / 2024 wild-rice / 2025 cold-tolerance rice pan-genomes, the PN40024 v5.1 grapevine reannotation, Lovell-2D/5D peach v3, Pusa Ruby tomato SL5.0 derivatives, recent Cannabis pangenomes, Santiago 2025 strawberry, Soybean Wm82.v4/v6 reannotations, and Col-CEN v1.2 bulk TE GFF (Naish 2021) — **all must be excluded or filtered from ground truth**. Only the older, manually-anchored libraries (most finalized 2008–2018) and a handful of lab-specific curated efforts (Wicker, PGSB, Schmidt/Heitkam, Ma/SoyTEdb, Jiao/Stitzer maize-v4, Jouffroy tomato, Daccord apple) meet the user's quality criterion.

## 1. Arabidopsis thaliana (and A. lyrata, A. arenosa)

### 1.1 TAIR10 TE annotation — the canonical Col-0 ground truth
- **Citation:** Buisine N, Quesneville H, Colot V. "Improved detection and annotation of transposable elements in sequenced genomes using multiple reference sequence sets." *Genomics* 91:467–475 (2008). DOI: 10.1016/j.ygeno.2008.01.005. Integrated into TAIR by Swarbreck et al. (2008) *NAR* 36:D1009; Lamesch et al. (2012) *NAR* 40:D1202.
- **Organism/assembly:** *A. thaliana* Col-0, TAIR10 (assembly unchanged since TAIR9, Nov 2010).
- **Data:** GFF3 (`TAIR10_GFF3_genes_transposons.gff`; ~31,189 TE loci) + `TAIR10_TE.fas` consensus library; ~326 families.
- **Curation:** Semi-automated REPET/TEannot pipeline with manual reconciliation against Repbase; curated by TAIR.
- **Classification:** Repbase-style + Wicker 2007 superfamilies; custom AT[chr]TE[id] identifiers.
- **Download:** https://www.arabidopsis.org/download/index-auto.jsp?dir=/download_files/Genes/TAIR10_genome_release/TAIR10_transposable_elements ; INRAE mirror https://urgi.versailles.inrae.fr/Data/Transposable-elements
- **Last update:** Nov 2010 (TE content is 2008/2010 vintage); Araport11 (Mar 2021) inherits the same coordinates.
- **Caveats:** TAIR10 lacks centromeres/rDNA, so centromeric ATHILA is absent; library is ~15 years old; some fragments misannotated as pseudogenes.
- **Ground-truth suitability:** **High** for Col-0 chromosome arms — the widely accepted community gold standard (used by Flutre 2011, Panda & Slotkin 2020, Quadrana 2016). **EDTA-independent.**

### 1.2 Col-CEN v1.2 — manual ATHILA layer only
- **Citation:** Naish M, Alonge M, Wlodzimierz P, et al. "The genetic and epigenetic landscape of the Arabidopsis centromeres." *Science* 374:eabi7489 (2021). DOI: 10.1126/science.abi7489.
- **Assembly:** Col-CEN v1.2 (near-T2T; GCA_028009825.1).
- **Data/curation:** Bulk TE GFF was generated by **EDTA v1.9.6** (with TAIR10 library as `--curatedlib`) and must be excluded. Layered on top is a **manually curated centromeric ATHILA GFF** (~53 centromeric + 58 arm ATHILA + solo LTRs, subfamilies ATHILA1–8) — this manual layer is EDTA-independent and usable.
- **Download:** https://github.com/schatzlab/Col-CEN (v1.2/).
- **Suitability:** Use **only the hand-curated ATHILA subset**; discard the `intact_repeat` / `EDTA.TEanno` layer.

### 1.3 Wlodzimierz et al. 2023 pancentromere ATHILA catalog
- **Citation:** Wlodzimierz P, Rabanal FA, Burns R, et al. "Cycles of satellite and transposon evolution in Arabidopsis centromeres." *Nature* 618:557–565 (2023). DOI: 10.1038/s41586-023-06062-z.
- **Scope:** 66 A. thaliana + 2 A. lyrata centromere assemblies; manually curated intact and solo ATHILA elements with subfamily assignment.
- **Download:** https://github.com/vlothec/pancentromere.
- **Suitability:** **High** for ATHILA-specific and cross-accession centromere benchmarking; one of the very few manually curated A. lyrata resources.

### 1.4 Repbase Arabidopsis (athrep)
- **Citation:** Bao W, Kojima KK, Kohany O. "Repbase Update." *Mob DNA* 6:11 (2015). DOI: 10.1186/s13100-015-0041-9.
- **Data:** Manually curated consensus FASTA (ATCOPIA, ATGP, ATHILA, ATLINE, ATREP, ATENSPM, ATMU, ATDNA, ATHATN families).
- **Download:** https://www.girinst.org/server/RepBase/ (subscription since 2018).
- **Suitability:** **High** family-level manual-curation reference; subscription-gated.

### 1.5 Dfam 3.9 curated Arabidopsis families
- **Citation:** Storer J, Hubley R, Rosen J, Wheeler TJ, Smit AFA. *Mob DNA* 12:2 (2021). DOI: 10.1186/s13100-020-00230-y.
- **Access:** Open; `famdb.py families --curated 3702` retrieves Viridiplantae taxid-filtered curated entries (largely mirrors Repbase content).
- **URL:** https://dfam.org; FamDB at https://github.com/Dfam-consortium/FamDB.
- **Suitability:** **Medium-high** for HMM-based benchmarking; filter out uncurated DR records.

### 1.6 Panda & Slotkin 2020 transcript-based overlay
- **Citation:** Panda K, Slotkin RK. "Long-Read cDNA Sequencing Enables a 'Gene-Like' Transcript Annotation of Transposable Elements." *Plant Cell* 32:2687–2698 (2020). DOI: 10.1105/tpc.20.00115.
- **Data:** BED/GFF covering 1,292 expressed TEs, preserving TAIR10 coordinates with added TSS/poly(A)/strand info.
- **URL:** https://github.com/KaushikPanda1/AthalianaTETranscripts.
- **Suitability:** Supplementary; good for validating expression-active TE coordinates.

### 1.7 A. lyrata RepetDB (Quesneville 2018)
- **Citation:** Quesneville H. "Transposable element annotation of Arabidopsis lyrata subsp. lyrata" (2018). DOI: 10.15454/INYUVZ.
- **Assembly:** A. lyrata MN47 v1.0.
- **Curation:** Explicitly **NOT manually curated** — raw REPET output. Most widely used A. lyrata TE reference in the literature.
- **URL:** https://entrepot.recherche.data.gouv.fr/dataset.xhtml?persistentId=doi:10.15454/INYUVZ.
- **Suitability:** Low-medium; flag non-curated status.

### 1.8 Family-specific references (ONSEN, Evadé, ATHILA, ATLINE, Sadhu, TSCL)
Manually curated individual references: **ONSEN/ATCOPIA78** (Ito 2011 *Nature* 472:115; Cavrak 2014 *PLoS Genet* 10:e1004115); **Evadé/ATCOPIA93** (Mirouze 2009 *Nature* 461:427; Mari-Ordóñez 2013 *Nat Genet* 45:1029); **ATHILA4** (Wright & Voytas 2002 *Genome Res* 12:122); **Sadhu** (Rangwala 2006 *PLOS Genet* 2:e36). All accessible via Repbase/Dfam or GenBank. **High** suitability as family-specific positive controls.

## 2. Rice (Oryza sativa) and related Oryza species

### 2.1 RiTE-db — the most comprehensive genus-wide resource
- **Citation:** Copetti D, Zhang J, El Baidouri M, et al. "RiTE database: a resource database for genus-wide rice genomics and evolutionary biology." *BMC Genomics* 16:538 (2015). DOI: 10.1186/s12864-015-1762-3.
- **Scope:** 11 diploid Oryza species + *Leersia perrieri* outgroup; 265,549 full-length TEs across six major superfamilies plus a literature-compiled manually curated tier.
- **Curation:** Tier-1 manually curated; tier-2/3 semi-automated from genome assemblies.
- **Classification:** Wicker 2007 three-letter code, cross-referenced to Osr/RIRE/Tos/Dasheng nomenclature.
- **URL:** http://www.genome.arizona.edu/rite (intermittently offline — consider Wayback Machine snapshots).
- **Suitability:** **High** for the manually-curated tier; **primary non-EDTA cross-species Oryza ground truth**.

### 2.2 RAP-DB TE annotation (Nipponbare IRGSP-1.0)
- **Citation:** Sakai H, Lee SS, Tanaka T, et al. *Plant Cell Physiol* 54:e6 (2013). DOI: 10.1093/pcp/pcs183. Assembly: Kawahara Y et al. *Rice* 6:4 (2013).
- **Data:** Repeat-masked genome + TE locus annotations integrated with gene GFF3 (no stand-alone FASTA library; RepeatMasker-based on legacy TIGR/MSU Oryza Repeats + Repbase).
- **URL:** https://rapdb.dna.affrc.go.jp/download/irgsp1.html.
- **Suitability:** Moderate — community-consensus coordinate reference for Nipponbare.

### 2.3 MSU Rice Genome Annotation Project (osa1_r7)
- **Citation:** Ouyang S, Zhu W, Hamilton J, et al. *NAR* 35:D883 (2007); Hamilton JP, Li C, Buell CR. *NAR* 53:D1614 (2025). DOI: 10.1093/nar/gkae1061.
- **Data:** `osa1_r7.asm.repeat_masked.gff3.gz` + TE-related gene models; homology-based from the MSU Oryza Repeat Database.
- **URL:** https://rice.uga.edu/download_osa1r7.shtml.
- **Suitability:** Moderate-high for Nipponbare MSU7 coordinates; note the homology library is the ancestor of the EDTA rice library, but the MSU7 GFF3 itself is independent of EDTA.

### 2.4 TIGR/MSU Plant/Oryza Repeat Database
- **Citation:** Ouyang S, Buell CR. *NAR* 32:D360 (2004). DOI: 10.1093/nar/gkh099.
- **Data:** Curated consensus FASTA; static since ~2009. Accessible at https://rice.uga.edu/annotation_oryza.shtml and JCVI BLAST.
- **Suitability:** Moderate historical reference; foundational seed for most downstream rice libraries.

### 2.5 RetrOryza — manually curated LTR retrotransposons
- **Citation:** Chaparro C, Guyot R, Zuccolo A, Piégu B, Panaud O. *NAR* 35:D66 (2007). DOI: 10.1093/nar/gkl780.
- **Data:** 242 manually curated rice LTR-RT reference molecules (Osr1–34, Retrosat1, RIRE1–10, Dagul, Hopi, Houba, Dasheng, Spip, Squiq).
- **URL:** http://www.retroryza.org (intermittently offline; sequences retrievable via GenBank accessions).
- **Suitability:** **High** for LTR-RT-only benchmarking.

### 2.6 McCarthy Osr and Ma-Bennetzen OAL libraries
- **Citations:** McCarthy EM, Liu J, Lizhi G, McDonald JF. *Genome Biol* 3:RESEARCH0053 (2002); Ma J, Devos KM, Bennetzen JL. *Genome Res* 14:860 (2004); Zhang W et al. *G3* 7:1875 (2017, OAL001–OAL790 across 8 AA-genome Oryza species).
- **Suitability:** **High** manually-curated LTR-RT ground truth; integrated into RiTE-db.

### 2.7 P-MITE database (rice MITE coverage)
- **Citation:** Chen J, Hu Q, Zhang Y, Lu C, Kuang H. *NAR* 42:D1176 (2014). DOI: 10.1093/nar/gkt1000.
- **Data:** ~339 rice MITE families / 179,415 elements; families manually annotated.
- **URL:** http://pmite.hzau.edu.cn / https://www.pmite.cn.
- **Suitability:** **High** MITE-specific ground truth.

### 2.8 Family-specific references: mPing/Ping/Pong, Dasheng/RIRE2, Tos17, CRR, hipa/houba/hopi/dagul, Spip/Squiq
Individually manually curated in GenBank with classical references (Jiang et al. 2003 *Nature* 421:163; Jiang et al. 2002 *Genetics* 161:1293 & *Plant Physiol* 130:1697; Hirochika 1996 *PNAS* 93:7783; Nagaki 2005 *MBE* 22:845; Panaud 2002 *MGG* 268:113; Vitte 2007 *Plant Sci* 172:8). **Very high** suitability as positive controls.

### 2.9 Stein et al. 2018 Oryza 13-genomes (non-EDTA)
- **Citation:** Stein JC, Yu Y, Copetti D, et al. *Nat Genet* 50:285 (2018). DOI: 10.1038/s41588-018-0040-0.
- **Data:** RepeatMasker using MIPS + AGI + RiTE-db libraries across 11 diploid Oryza species + IR8 + Nipponbare.
- **URL:** Gramene Oryza https://oryza.gramene.org.
- **Suitability:** **High** cross-species non-EDTA Oryza ground truth.

### 2.10 Zhang et al. 2016 ZS97RS1 / MH63RS1 (non-EDTA)
- **Citation:** Zhang J et al. *PNAS* 113:E5163 (2016). DOI: 10.1073/pnas.1611012113.
- **Data:** RepeatMasker with Repbase + P-MITE — **not EDTA**.
- **Suitability:** **Acceptable** ground truth for indica references.

### 2.11 ZS97RS3 / MH63RS3 (Song 2021)
- **Citation:** Song JM et al. *Mol Plant* 14:1757 (2021). TE-related genes from TBLASTN vs MIPS-REdat Poaceae v9.3p (not EDTA).
- **URL:** http://rice.hzau.edu.cn/rice_rs3/.
- **Suitability:** Moderate (gene-level only).

## 3. Maize (Zea mays)

### 3.1 Maize TE Consortium (MTEC) library — canonical FASTA
- **Citations:** Schnable PS et al. *Science* 326:1112 (2009); Baucom RS et al. *PLoS Genet* 5:e1000732 (2009); Du C et al. *PNAS* 106:19916 (2009); Wessler–Bennetzen 2015 consolidation.
- **Data:** Consensus FASTA (`maizeTE02052020`, `TE_12-Feb-2015_15-35.fa`) covering LTR/LINE/SINE/TIR/MITE/Helitron families across MTEC contributors (Jiang, Wessler, Bennetzen, SanMiguel, Dooner, Lisch).
- **Classification:** MTEC three-letter (a Wicker-2007 variant: RLG, RLC, RIL, RIX, RST, DTA, DTC, DTH, DTM, DTT, DHH).
- **URL:** https://github.com/oushujun/MTEC (library is pre-EDTA community curation; repository hosted by EDTA author but content is independent).
- **Suitability:** **Excellent** consensus FASTA — the gold standard maize TE library.

### 3.2 Jiao et al. 2017 B73 RefGen_v4 structural TE annotation (non-EDTA)
- **Citation:** Jiao Y, Peluso P, Shi J, Liang T, Stitzer MC, ..., Ware D. "Improved maize reference genome with single-molecule technologies." *Nature* 546:524–527 (2017). DOI: 10.1038/nature22971.
- **Data:** 130,604 intact LTR-RTs, 14,041 TIR copies, 21,095 Helitron copies; custom LTRharvest + LTRdigest + TARGeT pipeline anchored on MTEC library.
- **URL:** https://mcstitzer.github.io/maize_TEs/; https://github.com/mcstitzer/maize_v4_TE_annotation; Gramene FTP `zea_mays/repeat_annotation/B73v4.TE.filtered.gff3.gz`.
- **Suitability:** **Excellent** primary B73 ground-truth GFF — pre-dates EDTA and uses a custom structural pipeline.

### 3.3 Stitzer et al. 2021 refined B73 v4 annotation
- **Citation:** Stitzer MC, Anderson SN, Springer NM, Ross-Ibarra J. *PLoS Genet* 17:e1009768 (2021). DOI: 10.1371/journal.pgen.1009768.
- **Data:** Refined `B73.structuralTEv2.fulllength.2018-09-19.gff3` + `disjoined` version; nesting reconstructed; improved TIR/soloLTR/LINE boundaries.
- **URL:** https://mcstitzer.github.io/maize_TEs/; per-family Shiny: https://mcstitzer.shinyapps.io/maize_te_families/.
- **Suitability:** **Excellent** refined B73 v4 ground truth.

### 3.4 Springer et al. 2018 W22 (non-EDTA)
- **Citation:** Springer NM et al. "The maize W22 genome provides a foundation for functional genomics and transposon biology." *Nat Genet* 50:1282 (2018). DOI: 10.1038/s41588-018-0158-0.
- **Data:** >177,000 structural TEs in 26,833 families (Jiao-method + Helitron).
- **URL:** MaizeGDB FTP https://ftp.maizegdb.org/MaizeGDB/FTP/W22_v2.0/`W22.allTE.withSolo_10Jan18.gff3.gz`.
- **Suitability:** **Excellent** W22 ground truth.

### 3.5 Anderson et al. 2019 nesTEd 4-genome annotation
- **Citation:** Anderson SN et al. *Plant J* 100:1008 (2019). DOI: 10.1111/tpj.14489.
- **Data:** Consistent structural GFFs for B73 v4, W22, Mo17 CAU-1.0, PH207 v1.
- **URL:** https://github.com/SNAnderson/maizeTE_variation.
- **Suitability:** **Excellent** multi-line non-EDTA ground truth.

### 3.6 Family-specific curated references
- **Baucom 2009** (>400 LTR/LINE/SINE families, manually inspected); **Du & Dooner 2009 / Yang & Bennetzen 2009** Helitrons (Cornucopious, HelA/B, 62 subfamilies / 498 exemplars); **Jiang/Wessler MITE families** (Heartbreaker/Hbr, Hb2, mPIF, Tourist, Stowaway; Zhang 2000 *PNAS*, Casa 2000 *PNAS*, Han 2013 *BMC Genomics*); **SanMiguel/Bennetzen LTR-RTs** (Huck, Prem1/2, Opie, Ji, Grande, Cinful-Zeon, Tekay, CRM, Reina); **Du & Dooner 2011 Ac/Ds** complete family catalog (*BMC Genomics* 12:588). All **high** suitability as family-level ground truth.

### 3.7 Repbase Zea mays (subscription)
827 manually curated Zea mays entries as of 2019 (Cinful, Huck, Opie, Ji, Grande, Prem1/2, CRM, Reina, Hopie, etc.). Accessible at https://www.girinst.org/repbase/ (subscription). **High** orthogonal manual-curation reference.

### 3.8 Gramene / Ensembl Plants B73 repeat track (non-EDTA)
RepeatMasker-based track using RepBase + the 12-Feb-2015 Wessler–Bennetzen library; available for v4 and v5 via FTP `ftp.ensemblgenomes.org/pub/plants/release-62/gff3/zea_mays/`. Useful non-EDTA homology track for v5 coordinates.

## 4. Other angiosperms

### 4.1 Wheat (Triticum aestivum) — TREP / ClariTeRep (gold-standard grass TE annotation)
- **Citations:** Wicker T, Matthews DE, Keller B. *Trends Plant Sci* 7:561 (2002); Wicker T, Gundlach H, Spannagl M, et al. "Impact of transposable elements on genome structure and evolution in bread wheat." *Genome Biology* 19:103 (2018). DOI: 10.1186/s13059-018-1479-0; Daron J et al. *Genome Biol* 15:546 (2014, CLARI-TE).
- **Assembly:** Chinese Spring IWGSC RefSeq v1.0 / v2.0 / v2.1.
- **Data:** ClariTeRep library (3,050 manually annotated Triticeae TEs + 1,717 TREP families); genome-wide CLARI-TE GFF3 (505 families, ~4M copies, ~85% of genome).
- **Curation:** **Manual, expert-curated** over >20 years (Wicker/Zurich + PGSB/Mayer).
- **Classification:** Wicker 2007.
- **URL:** https://trep-db.uzh.ch (v.2025); IWGSC data via URGI https://urgi.versailles.inrae.fr/download/iwgsc/.
- **Suitability:** **★★★★★** The single best manually curated genome-wide plant TE annotation available; recommended primary monocot ground truth.

### 4.2 Barley (Hordeum vulgare) — Morex V3 + TREP
- **Citations:** Mascher M et al. *Plant Cell* 33:1888 (2021); Monat C et al. *Genome Biol* 20:284 (2019, Morex V2); Jayakodi et al. *Nature* 588:284 (2020, pan-genome).
- **Assembly:** Morex V3 (GCA_904849725.1), plus 20-genotype pan-genome.
- **Curation:** TREP library + PGSB pipeline; BARE1 retrotransposon family extensively characterized.
- **URL:** e!DAL/IPK https://doi.ipk-gatersleben.de/DOI/b2f47dfb-47ff-4114-89ae-bad8dcc515a1/.
- **Suitability:** **★★★★★** — shares TREP curation lineage with wheat.

### 4.3 Sugar beet (Beta vulgaris) — BeetRepeats (Schmidt/Heitkam lab)
- **Citation:** Schmidt N, Maiwald S, Mann L, et al. "BeetRepeats: reference sequences for genome and polymorphism annotation in sugar beet and wild relatives." *BMC Research Notes* 17:351 (2024). DOI: 10.1186/s13104-024-06993-4.
- **Data:** Comprehensive FASTA of >1,000 manually characterized families — LTR retrotransposons, chromoviruses, BvhAT, VulMITEs, BNR LINEs, Vulmar mariners, endogenous pararetroviruses.
- **Curation:** **Manual**, built on 30+ years of Schmidt lab characterization.
- **URL:** Supplementary FASTA in the BMC Research Notes 2024 paper.
- **Suitability:** **★★★★★** Best dicot manually-curated library outside soybean; recommended primary dicot ground truth.

### 4.4 Soybean (Glycine max) — SoyTEdb (Williams 82)
- **Citation:** Du J, Grant D, Tian Z, et al. "SoyTEdb: a comprehensive database of transposable elements in the soybean genome." *BMC Genomics* 11:113 (2010). DOI: 10.1186/1471-2164-11-113.
- **Data:** 32,552 Class I retrotransposons + 6,029 Class II DNA transposons with TSDs and boundaries, on Wm82.a1; FASTA + GFF.
- **Curation:** **Manual** — authors state it was at publication time the most comprehensive manually-curated plant TE database.
- **URL:** https://legacy.soybase.org/soytedb/.
- **Suitability:** **★★★★★** — do not substitute Wm82.v4/v6 EDTA-based reannotations.

### 4.5 Brachypodium distachyon — RepetDB Bd21
- **Citations:** International Brachypodium Initiative. *Nature* 463:763 (2010); Amselem J et al. *Mobile DNA* 10:6 (2019).
- **Data:** REPET TEdenovo+TEannot consensus library + GFF3 on Bd21 v3.2.
- **Curation:** Semi-automated with manual review.
- **URL:** https://urgi.versailles.inrae.fr/repetdb; dataset DOI 10.57745/L2TBDE.
- **Suitability:** **★★★★** — standard Brachypodium reference.

### 4.6 Tomato (Solanum lycopersicum) — Jouffroy 2016 REPET library
- **Citations:** Jouffroy O, Saha S, Mueller L, Quesneville H, Maumus F. "Comprehensive repeatome annotation reveals strong potential impact of repetitive elements on tomato ripening." *BMC Genomics* 17:624 (2016). DOI: 10.1186/s12864-016-2980-z; Hosmani PS et al. *bioRxiv* 767764 (2019, SL4.0 re-run).
- **Assembly:** Heinz 1706 SL2.50 / SL4.0 — **avoid SL5.0 / TGG1.x pipeline, which uses EDTA-adjacent tools**.
- **Curation:** Three-method semi-automated (REPET + RepeatScout + Tallymer) with post-processing.
- **URL:** Sol Genomics Network ftp://ftp.solgenomics.net/tomato_genome/.
- **Suitability:** **★★★★** — best tomato reference; avoid newer EDTA-based tomato annotations.

### 4.7 Grapevine (Vitis vinifera) — PN40024 12X.v2 / V4 via REPET
- **Citations:** Jaillon O et al. *Nature* 449:463 (2007); Velt A et al. *G3* 13:jkad067 (2023, V4); Shi X et al. *Hort Res* 10:uhad061 (2023, T2T).
- **Data:** REPET-based TE annotation on 12X.v2/V4. **Flag:** PN40024 v5.1 annotation (Santiago 2024) uses EDTA — exclude.
- **URL:** Grapedia https://grapedia.org/genomes/; URGI https://urgi.versailles.inrae.fr/Species/Vitis; T2T at https://github.com/zhouyflab/PN40024_T2T_Genome.
- **Suitability:** **★★★** (12X.v2/V4 only).

### 4.8 Apple (Malus domestica) — GDDH13 REPET
- **Citation:** Daccord N et al. *Nat Genet* 49:1099 (2017). DOI: 10.1038/ng.3886.
- **Data:** REPET TEdenovo+TEannot on GDDH13 v1.1; ~57.3% of 650 Mb TE; HODOR retrotransposon discovery.
- **URL:** GDR https://www.rosaceae.org/species/malus/malus_x_domestica/genome_GDDH13_v1.1.
- **Suitability:** **★★★★** — best Rosaceae fruit-tree reference.

### 4.9 Peach (Prunus persica) — Lovell v2.0.a1 REPET
- **Citations:** International Peach Genome Initiative. *Nat Genet* 45:487 (2013); Verde I et al. *BMC Genomics* 18:225 (2017).
- **Data:** REPET consensus library with BLASTX/Censor-based manual curation against Repbase.
- **URL:** GDR https://www.rosaceae.org/species/prunus_persica/genome_v2.0.a1.
- **Caveat:** **Avoid 2025 Lovell 2D/5D v3 reannotation — uses EDTA.**
- **Suitability:** **★★★** (v2.0.a1 only).

### 4.10 Medicago truncatula — MtrunA17 v5.0
- **Citation:** Pecrix Y et al. *Nat Plants* 4:1017 (2018). DOI: 10.1038/s41477-018-0286-7.
- **Data:** ~25,000 TE annotations via REPET.
- **URL:** https://medicago.toulouse.inra.fr/MtrunA17r5.0-ANR/.
- **Suitability:** **★★★★** legume reference.

### 4.11 Banana (Musa acuminata) — v2
- **Citations:** D'Hont A et al. *Nature* 488:213 (2012); Martin G et al. *BMC Genomics* 17:243 (2016).
- **Data:** REPET-based TE annotation with manual mapping input (Flutre, Choulet).
- **URL:** Banana Genome Hub https://banana-genome-hub.southgreen.fr.

## 5. Multi-species / general databases

### 5.1 Repbase Update (GIRI)
- **Citation:** Bao W, Kojima KK, Kohany O. *Mobile DNA* 6:11 (2015); current release **31.02 (Feb 2026)**.
- **Access:** Subscription since April 2019 (Phoenix Bioinformatics); last free version = Update 24.03.
- **Data:** Manually curated consensus FASTA + EMBL; plant coverage is deepest for *Oryza*, *Arabidopsis*, *Zea*, *Triticum*, *Medicago*, *Sorghum*, *Gossypium*.
- **URL:** https://www.girinst.org/repbase/.
- **Suitability:** **Gold standard** for manually curated consensus sequences — but license prohibits external redistribution.

### 5.2 Dfam 3.9
- **Citation:** Storer J et al. *Mobile DNA* 12:2 (2021); 3.9 release = 4.1 M families across 2,784 taxa.
- **Plant curated content:** Only ~300 manually curated "DF" families in Viridiplantae (262 retro + 38 DNA); vast majority of plant entries are uncurated "DR" RepeatModeler2 records.
- **URL:** https://dfam.org (open, CC-BY).
- **Suitability:** High for model plants via DF records; use `--curated` filter.

### 5.3 PGSB-REdat / REcat v9.3p
- **Citations:** Nussbaumer T et al. *NAR* 41:D1144 (2013); Spannagl M et al. *NAR* 44:D1141 (2016).
- **Data:** ~62,000 sequences clustered at ≥95%/≥95%; strong on Triticeae, Brachypodium, sorghum, maize, Medicago, cotton, Brassica.
- **URL:** https://pgsb.helmholtz-muenchen.de/plant/recat/.
- **Suitability:** Good cereal/Triticeae ground truth. PGSB three-letter code (distinct from Wicker; hierarchically compatible).

### 5.4 TREP
See §4.1. URL https://trep-db.uzh.ch; **best manually-curated grass TE library**.

### 5.5 RepetDB (URGI/INRAE)
- **Citation:** Amselem J et al. *Mobile DNA* 10:6 (2019). URL https://urgi.versailles.inrae.fr/repetdb.
- **Data:** REPET/PASTEC semi-automated with transparent classification evidence; 23 species including A. thaliana, wheat, grape, Brachypodium, Medicago, apple, poplar, tomato, cacao, maize, rice.
- **Suitability:** Moderate; best-in-class transparency of classification evidence.

### 5.6 REXdb (RepeatExplorer lineage database)
- **Citation:** Neumann P, Novák P, Hoštáková N, Macas J. *Mobile DNA* 10:1 (2019).
- **Data:** 75,516 LTR-RT polyprotein domain sequences from 13,863 elements across 80 Viridiplantae species; 16 Copia + 14 Gypsy lineages (Ale, Angela, Bianca, Bryco, SIRE, TAR, Tork, Athila, CRM, Reina, Ogre/Retand, etc.).
- **URL:** http://repeatexplorer.org/?page_id=918; DANTE pipeline.
- **Suitability:** **Excellent** for LTR-RT lineage-level classification benchmarking.

### 5.7 GyDB 2.0
- **Citation:** Llorens C et al. *NAR* 39:D70 (2011). URL https://gydb.org.
- **Data:** Lineage-specific HMMs and alignments for Ty1/Copia, Ty3/Gypsy, Bel/Pao, Retroviridae, Caulimoviridae.
- **Suitability:** Lineage-level phylogenetic reference, not genome-wide.

### 5.8 InpactorDB
- **Citation:** Orozco-Arias S et al. *Genes* 12:190 (2021). Zenodo 10.5281/zenodo.4386316.
- **Data:** 67,241 non-redundant + 130,439 redundant plant LTR-RT consensuses across 195 species.
- **Caveat:** **Contains EDTA-sourced entries** — filter `source=EDTA` labels before use.
- **Suitability:** Usable after EDTA-filtering for lineage-level plant LTR-RT benchmarking.

### 5.9 MASiVEdb (Sirevirus database)
- **Citation:** Bousios A et al. *BMC Genomics* 13:158 (2012). URL http://bat.infspire.org/databases/masivedb/ (intermittent availability).
- **Data:** 16,243 intact Sireviruses from 11 plant genomes.
- **Suitability:** Narrow but high specificity for Sirevirus benchmark.

### 5.10 P-MITE
- **Citation:** Chen J, Hu Q, Zhang Y, Lu C, Kuang H. *NAR* 42:D1176 (2014).
- **Data:** ~2.3 M MITE sequences / 3,527 families across 41 plant species.
- **URL:** https://www.pmite.cn.
- **Suitability:** Best multi-species MITE reference.

### 5.11 TIGR Plant Repeat Databases (legacy)
- **Citation:** Ouyang S, Buell CR. *NAR* 32:D360 (2004).
- **Status:** Essentially static since 2007; data redistributed via PGSB-REdat / rice.uga.edu.
- **Suitability:** Legacy reference only.

### 5.12 Additional niche resources (for completeness)
- **MnTEdb** (mulberry): Ma B et al. *Database* 2015:bav004.
- **DPTEdb** (dioecious plants): Li SF et al. *Database* 2016:baw078.
- **CicerSpTEdb** (chickpea): Mokhtar MM et al. *PLoS ONE* 16:e0259540 (2021).
- **PlantLTRdb** (Mokhtar 2023, *Front Plant Sci* 14:1134627) — **uses EDTA; flag/filter**.
- **PlantRep** (Luo et al. 2022), **APTEdb** (Pedro et al. 2021), **Zhou 2021 plant LTR-RT dataset** (*Sci Data* 8:174) — largely automated; secondary.

### 5.13 TE curation protocol references (not databases but useful for scoring)
- **Goubert et al.** "A beginner's guide to manual curation of transposable elements." *Mobile DNA* 13:7 (2022).
- **Storer et al.** "Curation guidelines for de novo generated transposable element families." *Curr Protoc* 1:e154 (2021).

## 7. Known gaps and caveats

**No truly manually-curated genome-wide TE library exists** for: **(a) Arabidopsis post-TAIR10** — every T2T/pan-genome effort (Col-CEN, Col-CC/TAIR12, Kang 2023, Lian 2024, 1001G+) has reverted to EDTA; only ATHILA overlays are manually curated. **(b) The 25 non-B73 NAM founders** — Hufford 2021 and Ou 2024 are both EDTA-derived; only liftover of Jiao/Stitzer v4 to v5, or Ensembl RepeatMasker+MTEC tracks, are non-EDTA options. **(c) Sorghum** — no equivalent of TREP; McCormick 2018 is the best available. **(d) Most Oryza pan-genome assemblies** (Shang 2022, Qin 2021, Wu 2023, Zhou 2025) — EDTA/panEDTA only. **(e) Strawberry, Cannabis, newer tomato (SL5.0), newer peach (Lovell v3), newer grapevine (v5.1), newer soybean (Wm82.v4/v6)** — EDTA-reliant. **(f) A. arenosa** — no native curated library; literature uses the A. lyrata RepetDB proxy.

**Helitron ground truth is universally weak** across all plant genomes because Helitrons lack TIRs/TSDs; Du 2009 / Yang-Bennetzen 2009 maize Helitrons and AtREP entries in Repbase remain the best references, and any CARP Helitron F1 evaluation should treat truth sets as noisy.

**Repbase licensing** (subscription since April 2019) prohibits redistribution of derived libraries; plan internal-only benchmarking or use Dfam curated records as a free substitute. **Several key portals are intermittently offline** — RiTE-db (genome.arizona.edu/rite), RetrOryza, MASiVEdb, GrTEdb — consider archiving FASTAs via Wayback Machine or GenBank for reproducibility. **Classification heterogeneity** across sources (Wicker 2007 three-letter vs PGSB three-letter vs Repbase vs RAP/MSU custom) requires a harmonization layer; Wicker 2007 is the recommended canonical target.

**MTEC contamination:** at least one family (`DTA_ZM00081_consensus`) is known to contain LTR sequence and should be masked from F1 calculations (Munasinghe 2023 *PLoS Genet*). Several legacy maize coordinates are on B73 v1 only and require liftover.

## 8. Explicit "avoided" / EDTA-derived exclusion list

Per the user's instruction, the following datasets and derivatives have been actively excluded as **EDTA-generated, EDTA-bundled, or primarily EDTA-branded output**. The user should double-check nothing from this list has slipped into their benchmark:

**Original EDTA artifacts:**
- **Ou et al. 2019 Genome Biology** EDTA benchmark datasets (20:275, doi:10.1186/s13059-019-1905-y), including `Rice_MSU7.fasta.std6.9.5.out`, `Rice_MSU7.fasta.std7.0.0.out`, `rice 6.9.5.liban`, `rice 7.0.0.liban`, and the associated Drosophila/maize benchmark files.
- **Jiang_rice6.9.lib_LTR** from Ou & Jiang 2018 *Plant Physiol* 176:1410 (LTR_retriever paper) — direct predecessor of the EDTA rice library.
- Any TE library file identifiable as `*.EDTA.TEanno.gff3`, `*.EDTA.TElib.fa`, `*.mod.EDTA.*`, or `panEDTA.*`.

**Arabidopsis:**
- **Col-CEN v1.2 bulk TE GFF** (Naish 2021) — EDTA v1.9.6 output; retain only the separate manual ATHILA overlay.
- **TAIR12-TE / Col-CC TE GFF** (Ou et al., PAG31 2024; GitHub oushujun/TAIR12-TE; file `GCA_028009825.2_Col-CC_genomic.fna.mod.EDTA.TEanno.*.gff3`) — primary annotation is EDTA; exclude.
- **Lian et al. 2024** 69-accession pan-genome TE annotation (*Nat Genet* 56:982) — EDTA.
- **Kang et al. 2023** 32-accession pan-genome (*Nat Commun* 14:6259) — EDTA + Repbase.
- **1001G+ project** (bioRxiv 2024.12.23.629943) if and when it releases EDTA-based TEs.

**Rice:**
- **Zhou et al. 2020 PSRefSeq** (*Sci Data* 7:113) — uses `rice 7.0.0.liban` (EDTA lineage).
- **Shang et al. 2022** *Cell Research* 32:878 super pan-genome (251 accessions) — EDTA v1.9.6.
- **Qin et al. 2021** *Cell* 184:3542 (33 accessions) — EDTA/LTR_retriever lineage.
- **Wu et al. 2023** *Genome Biol* 24:179 syntelog pan-genome — EDTA.
- **Castanera et al. 2023** *eLife* 12:e86324 rice pan-TE — EDTA-derived.
- **2024 Nat Commun** wild-rice super-pangenome (Shenton et al.) — EDTA.
- **2025 Nat Commun** cold-tolerance rice pangenome — panEDTA.
- **Zhou et al. 2025** *Nature* 639:390 wild-cultivated rice pangenome — likely EDTA; verify.
- **Chen 2023 Mo17 T2T** rice-adjacent claim is maize — see maize list.

**Maize:**
- **Hufford et al. 2021** *Science* 373:655 NAM founders TE GFF3s for all 26 genomes (B73 NAM-5.0 + 25 founders) — EDTA v1.9.0 with MTEC as `--curatedlib`.
- **Ou et al. 2024** *Genome Res* 34:1140 panEDTA pangenome library — panEDTA.
- **MaizeGDB Box folders** containing `*EDTA.TEanno.gff3` (e.g., https://ars-usda.app.box.com/v/maizegdb-public/folder/176297337613 and /176298403241) — EDTA.
- **Bornowski et al. 2021** *Plant Genome* 14:e20114 stiff-stalk TE annotations (PHB47, PHJ40, LH82, LH145, PHN11) — EDTA v1.9.0.
- **Chen et al. 2023** *Nat Genet* 55:1221 Mo17 T2T (Zm-Mo17-REFERENCE-CAU-T2T-1.0) repeat annotation — EDTA + RepeatMasker.
- **Stitzer et al. 2024 / PanAnd teosinte** TE annotations — panEDTA.
- **Liu et al. 2025** *Genome Biol* 26:2025 B-chromosome assembly — EDTA-using.

**Other angiosperms:**
- **PN40024 grapevine v5.1** (Santiago 2024 reannotation) — EDTA.
- **Lovell 2D/5D peach v3 (2025)** — EDTA.
- **Tomato SL5.0 / Pusa Ruby / TGG1.x** derivatives using EDTA — EDTA.
- **Fragaria vesca Santiago 2025** and **F. × ananassa 'Seolhyang'** (*Sci Data* 2025) — EDTA + DeepTE.
- **Cannabis sativa 2025 pangenome** (*Nature*, 193 genomes) — EDTA.
- **Soybean Wm82.v4 / Wm82.v6 EDTA-based reannotations** — EDTA (SoyTEdb on Wm82.a1 remains usable).
- **PlantLTRdb** (Mokhtar 2023 *Front Plant Sci* 14:1134627) — uses EDTA; if used, filter source=EDTA entries.
- **InpactorDB** (Orozco-Arias 2021 *Genes* 12:190) — contains EDTA-derived sequences among other sources; filter by `source` label.

**General principle:** treat any plant genome or pan-genome paper from ~2020 onward as EDTA-using by default, and only include its TE annotation in ground truth after verifying the Methods section explicitly excludes EDTA/panEDTA/LTR_retriever-as-sole-annotator lineages. The gold-standard curated libraries almost all pre-date EDTA (2008–2018) and originate from the Wicker, PGSB/Mayer, Schmidt/Heitkam, Ma/SoyTEdb, Wessler, Bennetzen, Jiao/Stitzer, Quesneville/Maumus, or Jurka/Kapitonov groups.
